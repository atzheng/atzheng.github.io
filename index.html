About me

1 Bio

I’m Andy Zheng, a PhD student at the Operations Research Center at MIT, advised by Prof. Vivek Farias. I’m interested in the interplay between statistics and optimization – specifically, how to make optimal decisions in dynamic, uncertain environments.

Previously, I was a data scientist at Uber, working on match optimization for UberPOOL. I recieved a B.S. in Industrial Engineering and an M.S. in Computer Science at Northwestern.

In my spare time I play jazz (sax, piano), look for birds, and procrastinate by tweaking my Emacs config or the CSS on this site.

Please reach out on Twitter or at atz@mit.edu!

2 Publications

  • Adaptive Stochastic First-Order Optimization via Online Regularization (2020, Preprint) with Vivek Farias and Ciamac Moallemi.

    A general framework for adaptive gradient optimization (a la Adam, Adagrad, etc.), inspired by James-Stein estimation. Highlights include minimax convergence rates for SGD, and an application to stochastic proximal gradient descent.

  • The Limits to Learning an SIR Process (2020, Preprint) with Jackie Baek, Vivek Farias, Andreea Georgescu, Retsef Levi, Tianyi Peng, Deeksha Sinha, and Joshua Wilde.

    A Cramer-Rao bound for the stochastic SIR model, demonstrating the difficulty of learning such models early in a pandemic. In practice, we use regularization to reduce the variance of the estimator, and apply this approach to the spread of COVID-19.

  • Computing Estimators of Dantzig Selector type via Column and Constraint Generation (2019, Preprint) with Rahul Mazumder and Stephen Wright.

    Fast algorithms for the Dantzig selector and related problems, using large-scale LP techniques and warm starts.
